# HerHelp TrainLayer AI

## One-Page Product Paper

### Overview

**HerHelp TrainLayer AI** is an education-based no-code AI training service that helps non-technical teams learn practical AI concepts, prepare their own data, build specialized AI assistants, validate quality and safety, deploy into real workflows, and improve over time.

Many organizations want to use AI, but they lack AI engineers, data scientists, clean data pipelines, evaluation methods, privacy workflows, or deployment experience. They may have valuable documents, spreadsheets, forms, rules, SOPs, service knowledge, customer records, and internal workflows, but they do not yet know how to safely turn that knowledge into useful AI systems.

TrainLayer AI solves this gap by combining practical AI education with guided no-code AI training. It is not only a course platform and not only an AI builder. It is a learning-to-building environment that helps organizations become capable of understanding, adapting, validating, and operating their own specialized AI assistants.

---

## Core Positioning

**Learn AI by building your own specialized AI assistant.**

TrainLayer AI helps teams move from AI curiosity to real AI implementation.

**One-line product promise:**

> **TrainLayer AI helps non-technical teams turn their own documents, sheets, forms, and workflows into safe, specialized AI assistants.**

---

## The Problem

AI adoption is growing quickly, but many organizations still face a practical capability gap.

Common problems include:

- teams do not understand how AI systems work
- valuable organizational knowledge is trapped in documents, sheets, forms, and staff experience
- data is messy, duplicated, outdated, or sensitive
- users do not know whether to use prompts, RAG, fine-tuning, extraction, classification, or forecasting
- AI outputs are not properly validated before deployment
- hallucination, privacy leakage, and unsupported answers create risk
- technical AI platforms are too complex for non-technical teams
- organizations become dependent on outside vendors instead of building internal AI capability

The problem is not only lack of tools. The deeper problem is lack of practical AI understanding, data readiness, validation workflow, and deployment confidence.

---

## The Solution

HerHelp TrainLayer AI provides a guided path for organizations to understand AI and build usable AI assistants from their own data.

It combines:

- practical AI learning
- data preparation
- no-code AI assistant building
- evaluation and validation
- privacy and governance
- deployment options
- feedback and improvement loops

TrainLayer AI helps users learn what they are building, why each step matters, and how to reduce risk before using AI in real operations.

## Core Framework

The TrainLayer AI workflow is:

> **Learn → Prepare → Train → Validate → Deploy → Improve**

### 1. Learn

Users learn the practical AI mechanics behind the assistant they want to build.

Learning topics include:

- what AI can and cannot do
- data quality and AI quality
- prompt behavior
- RAG and source-grounded answers
- document-based AI
- classification
- extraction
- forecasting
- hallucination
- privacy
- evaluation
- deployment models

The goal is not deep mathematics or programming. The goal is practical understanding that helps users design safer and more useful AI systems.

### 2. Prepare

Users prepare their own organizational data.

Supported data sources may include:

- PDF documents
- Word documents
- spreadsheets
- Google Sheets
- CSV files
- websites
- FAQs
- forms
- images
- database exports
- chat logs
- support logs

Preparation features include:

- data cleaning
- duplicate detection
- topic tagging
- document splitting
- sensitive data detection
- redaction support
- data quality scoring
- dataset versioning

### 3. Train

Users build or customize specialized AI assistants through guided no-code workflows.

Training modes may include:

- RAG-based knowledge assistants
- document question-answering assistants
- spreadsheet insight assistants
- classification assistants
- extraction assistants
- forecasting assistants
- prompt behavior configuration
- fine-tuning-style example learning
- human-reviewed correction datasets

### 4. Validate

Users test the AI before real deployment.

Validation features include:

- test question sets
- accuracy scoring
- source-grounding checks
- hallucination checks
- privacy checks
- cost estimation
- human review
- risk-level assessment
- before/after comparison

Validation is one of TrainLayer AI’s most important differences from simple AI chatbot builders.

### 5. Deploy

Users deploy the assistant into real usage surfaces.

Deployment options may include:

- internal chat interface
- website widget
- dashboard integration
- LINE
- Teams / Slack
- API endpoint
- AI agent interface
- developer-assisted integration

Simple deployments can be no-code. Deeper integration can be supported through API and technical partners.

### 6. Improve

Users improve the assistant after real usage.

Improvement features include:

- feedback collection
- answer correction
- human approval
- version control
- retraining workflow
- usage analytics
- audit logs
- performance monitoring
- privacy and permission updates

## First Training Modes

TrainLayer AI should start with three high-value training modes.

Users upload approved documents and create an assistant that answers based only on approved sources.

Examples:

- government service FAQ assistant

### 2. Spreadsheet Insight AI

Users upload structured data and create simple summaries, classifications, or forecasts.

Examples:

- sales summary assistant
- stock demand forecasting

- attendance risk support
- product performance assistant

### 3. Form and Document Extraction AI

Users upload forms, invoices, orders, or document examples and train AI to extract structured fields.

Examples:

- SME invoices
- order forms
- school registration documents
- government application forms
- administrative documents
