# Profit Participation Boundaries

## Executive Summary

FUZE treats profit participation as a **long-term platform design direction**, not an immediate promise and not a guaranteed payout system.

Future stablecoin profit participation, if implemented, is a separate framework subject to legal, accounting, treasury, technical, and transparency controls. FUZE does not present profit participation as guaranteed return, guaranteed payout, dividend, passive income, fixed yield, guaranteed quarterly income, guaranteed ROI, or token-holder income promise.

The purpose of this paper is to define safe public boundaries for discussing future profit participation while keeping the FUZE token, Platform Credits, stablecoin compensation, stablecoin payout systems, product revenue, and future participation frameworks clearly separated.

FUZE public principle:

**Profit participation remains a long-term platform design direction. It is not immediate and not guaranteed. Any future framework requires legal, accounting, treasury, technical, and transparency readiness.**

FUZE execution order is:

**Product usage first. Platform rails second. Broader ecosystem participation after that.**

Profit participation follows this order. FUZE does not lead with payout promises. FUZE first builds products, users, revenue, platform rails, credit usage, reporting systems, accounting logic, legal review, treasury controls, and transparency infrastructure.

FUZE v2 launch focus is **HerHelp.com** and **ZAGA.io**, supported by FUZE Core Platform rails and accelerated internally by **Botmad**.

## 3. Public Model

### 3.1 Economic Role Separation

Profit participation remains separate from FUZE token and Platform Credits.

| Layer | Asset / System | Role |
|---|---|---|
| FUZE token | Ecosystem participation asset | Holder alignment, participation eligibility, product-linked benefits, governance readiness |
| Platform Credits | Internal product usage rail | AI usage, SaaS features, games, tools, workflows, subscriptions |
| Stablecoin compensation | USDT / USDC / fiat-equivalent payments | Team, advisor, contributor, partner, contractor, and operational compensation |
| Stablecoin payout system | Future participation framework | Possible policy-defined profit participation, subject to review |
| Product revenue | Fiat, stablecoins, card payments, usage fees | Business revenue generated by FUZE products |

Core rule:

**FUZE token is not the payout asset. Platform Credits are not the payout asset. Stablecoin payouts, if implemented, are a separate future framework.**

### 3.2 What Profit Participation May Mean

Profit participation can eventually mean:

**A future platform mechanism where eligible participants can receive stablecoin distributions based on policy-defined distributable profit, subject to legal, accounting, treasury, technical, and transparency controls.**

Important terms:

| Term | Meaning |
|---|---|
| Eligible participant | Wallet or account that meets future eligibility rules |
| Eligibility snapshot | Future method to determine eligible holders or accounts |
| Distributable profit | Profit amount available for participation after accounting policy, reserves, costs, taxes, and governance rules |
| Payout ledger | Record of calculated stablecoin distributions |
| Stablecoin payout | USDT, USDC, or approved stablecoin distribution |
| Reporting period | Monthly, quarterly, annual, or other period defined by policy |
| Transparency report | Public or semi-public report supporting payout calculation |
| Foundation claim | Possible stablecoin claim by Foundation Vault if eligible under future framework |

### 3.6 Distributable Profit Definition

FUZE does not state that profit participation is based on gross revenue.

Future participation uses an accounting-defined concept.

Possible definition:

**Distributable profit is the portion of platform profit that remains after operating costs, infrastructure costs, taxes, reserves, reinvestment needs, treasury policy, legal obligations, and approved business expenses.**

Important boundaries:

- not all revenue is profit
- not all profit is distributable
- not every period has distributable profit
- reserves can be required
- legal/accounting rules can limit distributions
- product losses can reduce distributable amounts
- payout can be zero in some periods

Safe wording:

**Any future participation amount depends on policy-defined distributable profit and can be zero for a given period.**

### 3.8 Foundation Vault Role

The Foundation Vault is designed as a permanent principal reserve.

Foundation Vault FUZE principal does not move.

If a future profit participation framework is implemented, the Foundation Vault can be eligible to claim stablecoin payouts if the policy allows.

| Foundation Vault Component | Policy |
|---|---|
| FUZE principal | Permanently locked |
| FUZE transfer | Forbidden |
| FUZE sale | Forbidden |
| Liquidity use | Forbidden |
| Stablecoin claim | Possible only if future framework is implemented and approved |
| Stablecoin destination | Approved Foundation Operations Wallet |
| Reporting | Public claim record if implemented |

Public wording:

**Foundation Vault principal remains permanently locked. If future stablecoin participation is implemented and the Foundation Vault is eligible, only stablecoin proceeds can be claimed under approved policy; the FUZE principal does not move.**

### 3.12 Profit Participation Activation Phases

#### Phase 1 — Concept and Boundaries

FUZE defines safe language, separates token / credits / stablecoin payout systems, publishes high-level future direction, and avoids guarantees.

#### Phase 2 — Accounting and Legal Review

FUZE defines distributable profit, reviews jurisdiction issues, defines eligible entity structure, and defines tax/accounting treatment.

#### Phase 3 — Technical Architecture

FUZE designs eligibility snapshots, payout ledger, claim contract, reporting system, stablecoin vault, and Foundation Vault claim logic.

#### Phase 4 — Internal Simulation

FUZE simulates revenue periods, payout calculations, eligibility snapshots, claim flows, unclaimed payouts, Foundation Vault claims, and reporting outputs.

#### Phase 5 — Public Framework

FUZE publishes public framework details, eligibility boundaries, risk disclosures, reporting process, and contract architecture where appropriate.

#### Phase 6 — Activation if Approved

FUZE activates only if legal, accounting, treasury, technical, governance, and transparency readiness are satisfied.
