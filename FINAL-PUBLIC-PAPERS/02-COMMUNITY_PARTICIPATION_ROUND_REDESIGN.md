# 02. COMMUNITY PARTICIPATION ROUND REDESIGN

## Purpose

This document clarifies the redesigned FUZE Community Participation Round for FUZE v2.

The purpose is to replace the previous presale-style, discount-driven model with a more disciplined, product-first, compliance-aware, and transparency-first participation model.

The redesigned round should support long-term ecosystem alignment, real community participation, product usage, and controlled token circulation. It should not be marketed as a short-term speculative token sale or as a guaranteed return opportunity.

## Current FUZE v2 Context

FUZE v2 is a transparency-first AI SaaS platform moving from broad vision into disciplined product-first execution.

The active execution order is:

> Product usage first. Platform rails second. Broader ecosystem participation after that.

The first hero product directions are:

- HerHelp.com — Web2 AI SaaS for SMEs
- ZAGA.io — Web3 community utility and Token Utility OS

FUZE is also preparing a Private Strategic Seed Round for strategic investors, operator-angels, and early-stage partners. Any token-related exposure in the Seed Round must be handled only as a private, legally reviewed component of the investment structure. The Seed Round is not a public token sale.

The existing tokenomics table keeps a total supply of 500,000,000 FUZE. The current Community Participation / Community Presale allocation remains 110,000,000 FUZE, equal to 22.00% of total supply.

The current presale-style model used four pricing tiers:

| Previous Tier | Previous Price | Previous Allocation Logic | Issue |
|---|---:|---|---|
| Pioneer Round | $0.015 | First 15M tokens | Heavy discount / TGE multiple framing |
| Builder Round | $0.025 | Next 25M tokens | Still price-upside driven |
| Architect Round | $0.035 | Next 30M tokens | Still framed around future TGE value |
| Vanguard Round | $0.045 | Remaining tokens | Still presale ladder logic |
| TGE Reference | $0.055 | Reference price | Creates return expectation |

The previous model should be retired because it over-emphasizes discount, TGE comparison, and implied return. FUZE v2 should not use language such as TGE multiple, guaranteed upside, buy before listing, or discount-to-TGE return framing.

## Key Decision / Recommended Direction

The Community Participation Round should be redesigned as a product-first, compliance-aware participation system.

Recommended decision:

> FUZE should replace the old four-tier Community Presale model with a redesigned Community Participation Round focused on fair access, product readiness, ecosystem alignment, transparent smart contract rules, eligibility controls, and long-term vesting.

The round should not open before the following readiness conditions are met:

1. FUZE v2 public narrative is published.
2. HerHelp.com and ZAGA.io launch milestones are defined.
3. Community Participation Paper is updated.
4. Token utility roadmap is clarified.
5. Platform readiness and claim/vesting logic are reviewed.
6. Legal, compliance, jurisdiction, and risk language are reviewed.
7. Existing deployed contract state is audited and reconciled.
8. Existing participants, if any, are handled fairly.

The recommended official rename is:

> Community Participation Round

Not:

- ICO
- public token sale
- community presale
- buy before listing
- TGE discount round

## Detailed Policy / Framework

### 1. Keep the 110M Community Allocation, But Redesign Internal Use

The 110,000,000 FUZE community allocation should remain unchanged for now, but it should be divided into clearer sub-purposes.

Recommended structure:

| Sub-Bucket | Suggested Amount | % of Total Supply | Purpose | Recommended Model |
|---|---:|---:|---|---|
| Community Participation Round | 70,000,000 FUZE | 14.00% | Main community access after readiness | Market-aligned / reviewed participation with vesting |
| Product User Participation | 15,000,000 FUZE | 3.00% | HerHelp/ZAGA users, pilot users, early product participants | Earned or usage-linked, not sold by default |
| Community Contributors | 10,000,000 FUZE | 2.00% | Contributors, ambassadors, testers, moderators, ecosystem builders | Milestone-based or contribution-based |
| Community Reserve / Unsold | 15,000,000 FUZE | 3.00% | Future community programs, unsold reserve, governance-approved use | Locked reserve / later approved use |
| **Total** | **110,000,000 FUZE** | **22.00%** | **Community allocation** | **Transparent policy split** |

This keeps the original 110M allocation but makes the allocation more aligned with FUZE v2.

### 2. Replace Discount Tiers With Participation Categories

The previous tiers should no longer be price-discount tiers. If the names are reused, they should represent participation role or access category, not implied return.

Recommended model:

| New Category | Purpose | Access Logic | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Early Community Access | Existing supporters and early aligned participants | Eligibility-controlled | No TGE multiple language |
| Product User Access | HerHelp/ZAGA early users and pilot participants | Product-linked eligibility | May be earned or capped participation |
| Ecosystem Builder Access | contributors, ambassadors, communities, partners | Contribution / allowlist / milestone | May include lockup |
| Open Community Access | broader eligible community after readiness | jurisdiction/compliance-gated | Publicly visible but eligibility-controlled |

This makes the round more community- and product-aligned.

### 3. Participation Unlock Schedule

The active FUZE v2 decision is:

> 5% unlock at TGE, 60-day cliff, remaining 95% linear daily unlock over 12 months.

Recommended wording:

> FUZE’s Community Participation Round is designed for long-term ecosystem alignment, not short-term speculation. At TGE, 5% of purchased tokens become claimable. After TGE, there is a 60-day cliff before the remaining allocation begins to unlock. After the cliff, the remaining 95% unlocks gradually on a linear daily schedule over 12 months.

This should be the default for purchased Community Participation tokens.

### 4. Pricing Should Be Finalized Later

The new round should not publish final pricing until readiness is achieved.

Pricing should be finalized only after:

- Seed Round structure is clearer
- token utility documentation is updated
- legal/compliance review is complete
- market conditions are reviewed
- platform readiness is confirmed
- old contract reconciliation is complete

Recommended current placeholder:

> Pricing, eligibility, jurisdiction rules, vesting, and claim terms will be finalized in the updated Community Participation Paper after product, platform, legal, and compliance readiness review.

### 5. Existing Deployed Contract Handling

If a previous Community Presale contract has already been deployed, FUZE should not simply abandon or replace it without reconciliation.

Required steps:

1. Snapshot current contract state.
2. Record deposited tokens, sold tokens, buyers, payments, claims, vesting state, unsold tokens, and admin actions.
3. Determine whether there are existing participants.
4. If participants exist, define a fair policy: honor, refund, migrate, or opt-in to updated terms.
5. Withdraw unsold tokens only if contract rules allow and participant rights are not harmed.
6. Publish a careful update after review.
7. Recreate the contract only after updated rules are finalized.

Recommended public wording:

> FUZE is redesigning the Community Participation Round to align with FUZE v2’s product-first strategy, platform readiness, legal/compliance review, and updated tokenomics documentation. Existing participant records, if any, will be reviewed and handled fairly before any new round structure is launched.

### 6. Eligibility-Controlled Access

The public UI may be visible to everyone, but participation should be eligibility-controlled.

Use:

> eligible participants

Avoid:

> everyone can buy

Eligibility may depend on:

- jurisdiction
- wallet status
- community status
- product-user status
- allowlist status
- KYC/compliance requirements if needed
- purchase caps
- anti-bot controls
- legal review

### 7. Unsold Token Policy

Unsold tokens should not automatically move to a team, admin, or arbitrary treasury wallet.

Recommended policy:

> Unsold Community Participation tokens should return to the Community Reserve or remain locked under governance-approved future community use.

Unsold tokens may later support:

- future product-user programs
- community campaigns
- public vault access windows
- contributor rewards
- ecosystem-aligned participation
- governance-approved community reserve use

## Smart Contract / Technical Implications

The old contract should not be reused blindly.

A redesigned contract should support:

- clear participation windows
- eligibility control
- supported stablecoin payment tokens
- purchase caps
- vesting / claim schedule
- policy hash
- metadata URI
- public read functions
- event logs
- pause / emergency control
- multisig-controlled admin functions
- timelock for sensitive policy changes
- unsold token destination policy
- participant migration handling if replacing old contract

Recommended contract name:

> FuzeCommunityParticipationRound

or:

> FuzeCommunityParticipationVault

Recommended public read fields:

- total allocation
- available allocation
- sold allocation
- buyer purchased amount
- buyer claimed amount
- vesting start
- cliff end
- claimable amount
- payment token
- policy hash
- metadata URI
- round status

Recommended event examples:

- CommunityParticipationOpened
- CommunityParticipationPurchased
- CommunityParticipationClaimed
- CommunityParticipationPaused
- CommunityParticipationFinalized
- UnsoldTokensReturned
- PolicyHashUpdated
- EligibilityRootUpdated

The contract should not include unrestricted admin reassignment of user allocations or arbitrary withdrawal of participant-owned amounts.

## Risk and Compliance Notes

The Community Participation Round may create legal, regulatory, tax, investor-protection, and jurisdictional considerations.

FUZE should not publish final participation terms before legal review.

Key risk controls:

- do not market expected returns
- do not compare price to TGE as a profit multiple
- do not promise listing or liquidity
- do not use public token sale language where not legally reviewed
- define eligible participants carefully
- review jurisdiction restrictions
- provide risk notices
- document buyer terms
- implement purchase caps and anti-bot controls
- maintain public smart contract transparency

Recommended disclaimer-style language:

> Participation availability, pricing, eligibility, jurisdiction rules, and claim terms are subject to legal, compliance, product, and platform-readiness review. FUZE does not guarantee token price, liquidity, listing, profit, or return.
