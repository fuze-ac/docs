# Team / Advisor / Partner Vesting

## Executive Summary

FUZE uses long-term vesting and milestone-based release rules for team members, advisors, strategic contributors, ecosystem partners, and any private Seed Round token-related exposure where applicable.

The purpose is to align long-term builders, advisors, contributors, and partners with the FUZE ecosystem while reducing immediate liquid sell-pressure risk. FUZE does not use team, advisor, partner, or Seed-related token exposure as short-term extraction. FUZE uses vesting, cliffs, milestone conditions, lockups, revocation rules, stablecoin compensation separation, and aggregate public reporting to strengthen trust.

FUZE applies the principle:

**FUZE token grants align long-term builders, advisors, contributors, and partners with the ecosystem. Stablecoins pay for work where cash compensation is needed.**

This model supports FUZE v2’s Controlled Circulation Policy and gives investors, holders, community members, and strategic partners a clearer view of how insider, contributor, partner, and private strategic exposure remains controlled.

## 1. FUZE Position

FUZE uses long-term vesting for team, advisors, strategic contributors, ecosystem partners, and Seed token-related exposure where included.

FUZE position:

**Team, advisor, partner, and Seed token-related allocations are long-term aligned and do not create immediate liquid sell pressure.**

FUZE uses the following vesting and release model:

| Category | Schedule / Release Model |
|---|---|
| Team Allocation | 12-month cliff + 36-month linear vesting |
| Standard Advisor | 6-month cliff + 24-month linear vesting |
| Strategic Advisor | 12-month cliff + 36-month linear vesting |
| Strategic Contributor | Milestone-based vesting or 6-month cliff + 24-month vesting |
| Ecosystem Partner | Milestone-based release or market-aligned purchase with lockup |
| Seed token-related exposure, where included | 0% at TGE, 6-month cliff, 24-month linear vesting |

These schedules support FUZE v2’s product-first strategy, Controlled Circulation Policy, and stablecoin compensation model.

FUZE separates work payment from token alignment:

**Stablecoins pay for work. FUZE tokens align long-term participation. Platform Credits power product usage.**

## 3. Public Model

### 3.1 Vesting Definitions

| Term | Meaning |
|---|---|
| Cliff | Waiting period before tokens begin to vest or become claimable |
| Linear vesting | Tokens vest gradually over time after the cliff |
| Milestone-based vesting | Tokens vest after defined contribution, delivery, integration, or growth milestone |
| Revocation | Unvested tokens can be cancelled or returned if the recipient leaves, stops contributing, or violates terms |
| Lockup | Tokens are owned or allocated but cannot be transferred until a date or condition |
| Claim schedule | Schedule under which vested tokens can be claimed |
| Stablecoin compensation | USDT, USDC, or fiat-equivalent payment for work, separate from token alignment |

### 3.2 Team Allocation Vesting

| Item | Rule |
|---|---|
| Amount | 45,000,000 FUZE |
| Default schedule | 12-month cliff + 36-month linear vesting |
| Conservative alternative | 12-month cliff + 48-month linear vesting |
| Immediate unlock | No |
| Public buy access | Limited / exceptional only |
| Stablecoin compensation relevance | High |
| Primary purpose | Long-term builder and operator alignment |

Default team vesting means:

- no team tokens become claimable during the first 12 months
- after the 12-month cliff, team tokens vest gradually over 36 months
- total vesting period is 48 months from vesting start
- claimed tokens can remain subject to additional lockup or internal policy where needed

Team tokens are used for long-term builder and operator alignment. They are not treated as short-term salary.

Operational compensation can be handled through:

- stablecoins
- fiat-equivalent payments
- product revenue
- Seed Round funds
- approved treasury operations

Team revocation applies to unvested allocation where a team member:

- leaves before vesting
- fails to contribute
- violates agreement terms
- is removed for cause
- does not meet role requirements

Revoked unvested tokens return to a defined destination such as:

- Team Vesting Vault
- Treasury Reserve
- Future Contributor Reserve

Final destination is defined in legal and token grant agreements.

Public disclosure includes:

- total team allocation
- vesting schedule
- aggregate vested / unvested / claimed amounts
- no immediate large unlocks
- stablecoin compensation separation

Public disclosure excludes:

- individual salaries
- individual grant amounts unless voluntarily or publicly agreed
- personal wallet addresses unless needed for public registry
- private employment terms

### 3.5 Ecosystem Partner Release Rules

Ecosystem partners are separate from team and advisors.

Partners can receive tokens through:

- milestone-based grants
- market-aligned strategic purchase
- product/integration-based release
- distribution milestone release
- campaign-based release
- lockup-based strategic access

| Partner Type | Release Model |
|---|---|
| Integration partner | Milestone-based release after integration delivery |
| Distribution partner | Release based on user, community, or revenue milestones |
| Web3 ecosystem partner | Market-aligned purchase with lockup |
| Telegram community partner | Campaign-based release with caps |
| Technical partner | Milestone-based grant with vesting |
| Strategic commercial partner | Negotiated participation, lockup, and legal review |

Partner vesting or lockup defaults to:

**6–24 month lockup or vesting, depending on strategic value and token amount.**

If a partner receives any pricing benefit, longer lockup applies.

| Discount / Benefit | Minimum Lockup |
|---|---|
| Market price | Window-specific lockup |
| Small discount | 6–12 months |
| Medium discount | 12–24 months |
| Large discount | Exceptional and legally reviewed only |

Milestone-based partner grants include clawback or non-release where:

- integration is not delivered
- partnership is cancelled
- user or revenue milestone is not achieved
- partner violates agreement
- partner misuses FUZE brand
- compliance issue arises

FUZE does not publish private Seed token terms publicly before legal review and qualified private discussion.

### 3.8 Claim and Transfer Restrictions

FUZE separates:

- vesting
- claiming
- transferability

A token can be vested while still subject to claim conditions or transfer restrictions where legally or contractually required.

Recommended rules:

- no unrestricted immediate token access for team and advisors

- claims follow linear vesting schedule

## 6. Risk Boundaries and Safeguards

FUZE does not guarantee token price, liquidity, listing, profit, return, payout, or market performance.

FUZE applies the following safeguards through vesting and milestone-based release:

| Risk Area | FUZE Safeguard |
|---|---|
| Immediate insider liquidity | Team and advisor allocations use cliffs and linear vesting |
| Founder/team sell-pressure concern | Team allocation is long-term aligned and separated from salary compensation |
| Advisor title-only allocations | Advisor tokens are contribution-based and revocable if contribution stops |
| Strategic contributor misuse | Contributor grants are milestone-based, delayed, capped, or vested |
| Partner grant misuse | Partner releases are milestone-based, lockup-based, or market-aligned |
| Seed investor liquidity concern | Token-related exposure, where included, uses 0% at TGE, 6-month cliff, 24-month vesting |
| Compensation-driven selling | Stablecoins or fiat-equivalent payments compensate work where practical |
| Private term confusion | Private terms are not public token sale terms |
| Over-disclosure risk | Public reporting remains aggregate and category-level |
| Public return expectation | FUZE avoids ROI, discount-to-TGE, price target, listing-promise, and liquidity-promise framing |

FUZE does not guarantee exchange listing, listing timing, liquidity, price performance, or market outcome.

Vesting does not remove market risk. It defines long-term alignment, release discipline, contribution control, private-term boundaries, and public reporting expectations.
