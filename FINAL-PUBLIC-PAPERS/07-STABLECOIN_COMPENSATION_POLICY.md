# Stablecoin Compensation Policy

## Executive Summary

FUZE uses a **Stablecoin Compensation Policy** to separate work compensation from FUZE token circulation.

The policy supports FUZE v2’s Controlled Circulation Policy:

**Stablecoins pay for work. FUZE tokens align long-term participation.**

FUZE compensates team members, advisors, contributors, contractors, and selected partners through USDT, USDC, fiat-equivalent payments, milestone payments, product revenue, approved treasury operations, or approved Public Vault Access proceeds where applicable. FUZE token allocations remain focused on long-term ecosystem alignment, contribution recognition, governance readiness, product utility, and approved ecosystem participation.

This model reduces forced token selling, improves team sustainability, supports investor confidence, strengthens community trust, and keeps FUZE tokenomics aligned with a product-first AI SaaS platform.

## 1. FUZE Position

FUZE separates operational compensation from token circulation.

FUZE position:

**Stablecoins pay for work. FUZE tokens align long-term participation. Platform Credits power product usage.**

This means:

- team members receive stablecoin or fiat-equivalent compensation where practical
- advisors receive stablecoin milestone payments and vested FUZE only where contribution alignment applies
- contributors receive stablecoin payments, milestone bounties, Platform Credits, or capped token grants based on contribution type
- partners receive stablecoin payments for deliverables and token exposure only where long-term strategic alignment applies
- FUZE token allocations remain vested, locked, earned, milestone-based, or policy-controlled
- approved stablecoin proceeds from selected vault access windows can support approved compensation purposes

FUZE policy:

**FUZE compensates work through USDT, USDC, fiat-equivalent payments, milestone payments, product revenue, or approved treasury operations. FUZE token allocations are used primarily for long-term alignment, contribution recognition, ecosystem participation, governance readiness, and product utility.**

The Stablecoin Compensation Policy fits this platform model because FUZE builds real products, hires and rewards real contributors, and avoids using liquid token unlocks as the default operating payment method.

## 3. Public Model

### 3.1 Core Policy Statement

FUZE separates work compensation from token circulation.

Where team members, advisors, contributors, contractors, or partners require compensation for work, FUZE uses stablecoins, fiat-equivalent payments, milestone payments, product revenue, or approved treasury operations rather than relying on large unlocked FUZE token releases.

FUZE token allocations are used for long-term ecosystem alignment, contribution recognition, governance readiness, product utility, and approved ecosystem participation.

### 3.2 Compensation vs Token Alignment

| Category | Best Asset / Payment Type | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| Salary / contractor work | USDT, USDC, fiat-equivalent payment | Pay for labor, development, operations, support, design, legal, accounting, security |
| Advisory work | Stablecoin milestone payment + vested FUZE where appropriate | Reward real advisory contribution |
| Contributor work | Stablecoin payment, milestone bounty, Platform Credits, or vested grant | Reward delivery and contribution |

| Product usage | Platform Credits | Pay for AI usage, SaaS features, tools, workflows, games |
| Future profit participation | Stablecoin payout system, subject to legal, accounting, treasury, technical, and transparency readiness | Future framework only after readiness |

Core rule:

**FUZE does not release FUZE tokens when the real need is cash compensation.**

### 3.6 Compensation Flow Model

FUZE compensation flow:

1. Work or milestone is approved.
2. Compensation amount is defined in stablecoin or fiat-equivalent terms.
3. Payment source is identified.
4. Payment is approved through the appropriate internal process.
5. Payment is executed from an approved wallet or operating account.
6. Payment is recorded internally.
7. Aggregate category-level reporting appears in transparency updates where appropriate.
8. Individual compensation details remain private unless disclosure is required or approved.

Example flow:

| Step | Action |
|---|---|
| 1 | Developer completes milestone |
| 2 | Milestone approved |
| 3 | USDC payment amount confirmed |
| 4 | Payment approved by operations or multisig policy |
| 5 | Payment sent from approved operating wallet |
| 6 | Internal record updated |
| 7 | Public report shows category-level development compensation total, not private personal data |

### 3.7 Public Vault Access Proceeds for Compensation

Selected Public Vault Access windows can generate stablecoin proceeds.

Those proceeds can support approved compensation categories where policy allows.

Example:

A policy-approved Team Compensation Conversion Window opens. Eligible participants buy FUZE from the approved vault at market-aligned pricing. Stablecoin proceeds route to an approved Team Compensation Wallet or Treasury Operations Wallet. Purchased FUZE enters lockup or vesting based on the access-window rules.

Rules for this model:

- selected windows only
- clear source vault
- clear purpose
- market-aligned price
- purchase caps
- lockups for buyers
- public proceeds destination
- no hidden routing
- multisig or timelock where appropriate
- policy hash
- public event logs

## 4. Investor and Community Relevance

The Stablecoin Compensation Policy matters because it makes FUZE tokenomics more disciplined and more credible.

For investors, the policy shows that FUZE separates operating payments from token circulation. This reduces forced token selling, supports professional execution, improves compensation clarity, and strengthens the Controlled Circulation Policy.

For community members, the policy explains why FUZE avoids paying contributors only through liquid token unlocks. Stablecoin compensation reduces unnecessary sell pressure and protects the role of FUZE token as a long-term participation asset.

For team members and contributors, the policy creates clearer payment expectations. Work compensation uses stablecoin or fiat-equivalent value where practical, while FUZE token exposure remains long-term alignment.

For partners and advisors, the policy separates service delivery from strategic alignment. Stablecoins pay for deliverables. Vested or milestone-based FUZE exposure applies where long-term ecosystem alignment exists.

For product users, the policy preserves the distinction between Platform Credits and compensation payments. Platform Credits remain a product usage layer, not a payroll or investment layer.

FUZE is not relying on token speculation as the only exit path. FUZE is building real products, real users, revenue potential, shared platform rails, and strategic acquisition optionality.

FUZE does not guarantee token price, liquidity, listing, profit, return, payout, or market performance.
